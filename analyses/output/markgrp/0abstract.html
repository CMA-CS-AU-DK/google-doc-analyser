We are exploring how physicalizations based on group data can foster social interaction and shared reflect in families through three studies. From formative interviews conducted with families, we discovered that they have busy schedules, often only having quality time at dinnertime and in weekends. Afterward, Focus groups were conducted to explore the qualities of three physicalizations, Annotated Prototype, Laser Cut Prototype, and Interactive Prototype, based on group data from heart rate. The focus group showed that participant actively explored and discussed the physicalizations, creating fun narratives. Furthermore, from this study we deduced eight design considerations for physicalizations to foster social interaction and shared reflection on group data. Heart-Tale was designed based on the considerations to conduct take-home studies in family environments. Heart-Tale became a part of the dining room and was used nearly every day.
